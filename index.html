Overfishing

Overfishing occurs when fish and other marine species are caught at a rate faster than they can reproduce. This practice leads to the depletion of fish populations and disrupts marine ecosystems.

Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution is one of the most pressing environmental issues of our time. Millions of tons of plastic waste enter our oceans every year, harming marine life and contaminating the food chain.

Single-use plastics, such as plastic bags, straws, and water bottles, are major contributors to plastic pollution. These items are used for a few minutes, but take hundreds of years to decompose.

Consequences of Plastic Pollution:

  • Harms marine life, including sea turtles, whales, and fish
  • Contaminates the food chain, affecting human health
  • Contributes to climate change, as plastic production requires fossil fuels
  • Pollutes our oceans, rivers, and landscapes, affecting ecosystems and wildlife habitats

What Can We Do?

  • Reduce our use of single-use plastics
  • Recycle as much as possible
  • Participate in beach cleanups and community events
  • Support organizations working to address plastic pollution
